Introduction to Handlebars

As a general note, Handlebars is my personal favorite templating engine. It's one of many, actually—alongside Pug (F.K.A. Jade) it is one of the most popular solutions to having to type out all of your HTML by hand, barring Emmet abbreviations, macros, etc. Handlebars is what is known as a "Semantic Templating" framework for JavaScript and HTML. Frameworks like these are used as a replacement to constructing long strings of HTML within JavaScript code while also providing programmers with a simpler method through which to dynamically create or to fill HTML elements.

What makes Handlebars unique is its introduction of moustache brackets, e.g. {{FOO}}, to directly inject data into our front-end. No concatenation, no problem. It's slightly prettier than ES6 template strings as well. You can also use triple moustache brackets, e.g. {{

Ham, spam and eggs

}}, to insert unescaped HTML. (You can take a look at the Handlebars site to get a feel for this syntax if you're struggling.)

Other templating engines leverage similar ideas as well for the sake of scrubbing user input: if someone types in raw HTML, we don't have to worry about <script> tags and other nastiness.
